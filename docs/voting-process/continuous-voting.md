# Continuous Voting

## Principle

**Continuous voting** means that a citizen with a legal right to vote can **at any time** cast their vote on any issue put to a vote. There is no fixed election day — voting happens continuously.

If a citizen has previously voted for a certain option and now changes their mind, their vote **moves** from one option to another. Thus, the ratio of votes changes constantly, reflecting the current will of society.

## Two Types of Voting

### Electoral Voting

Purpose — to determine which candidate will hold an elected office.

**Mechanics:**

1. Citizens can vote for any candidate from the list at any time
2. The current vote ratio is published daily
3. When a candidate receives more votes than the incumbent official, an **electoral race** begins
4. During the race, **electoral points (EP)** are calculated daily — the difference between vote percentages
5. Points accumulate day by day
6. The candidate wins upon reaching the threshold (e.g., 3,700 EP) or loses if all points are lost

### Decisive Voting (on issues and laws)

Purpose — to make a decision on a specific issue or law.

**Mechanics:**

1. A citizen or official proposes an issue
2. The issue undergoes a technical review
3. To be put to a vote, the issue must gather support (e.g., 10% of voters)
4. After gathering support, continuous "for" / "against" voting begins
5. When a sufficient share of the electorate has voted (e.g., 60%), cumulative counting starts
6. The winner is determined upon reaching the EP threshold

## Cumulative Point System

The key mechanism of continuous voting is **cumulative electoral point (EP) counting**.

### Formula

Daily EP calculation for electoral voting:

```
EP(day) = P(candidate) - P(incumbent)
```

Where `P(X)` is the percentage of votes from the total number of voters for candidate X.

Accumulated points:

```
EP(accumulated) = Σ EP(day) for all days of the race
```

### Example

Consider the dynamics of an electoral race:

| Day | Candidate (%) | President (%) | Difference (EP) | Accumulated (EP) |
| --- | ------------- | ------------- | --------------- | ---------------- |
| 1   | 35            | 35            | start           | 0                |
| 2   | 36            | 34            | +2              | 2                |
| 3   | 37            | 34            | +3              | 5                |
| 4   | 36            | 35            | +1              | 6                |
| 5   | 35            | 35            | 0               | 6                |
| 6   | 33            | 36            | −3              | 3                |

As shown, the candidate's advantage can both increase and decrease. The race continues until one participant wins.

### Example Timelines at a 3,700 EP Threshold

| Candidate's Advantage | Required Duration   |
| --------------------- | ------------------- |
| 10%                   | ~370 days (~1 year) |
| 25%                   | ~148 days           |
| 50%                   | ~74 days            |
| 100%                  | ~37 days            |

## Publication of Results

Information about the current vote ratio is published **daily** and is accessible to all citizens. This ensures:

- **Transparency** — everyone sees the current balance of power
- **Predictability** — trends in support changes are visible well in advance
- **Accountability** — incumbent officials see their level of support in real time

## Advantages

1. **Constant feedback** from society to government
2. **Rapid response** to changes in society
3. **No "dead period"** — citizens influence power every day
4. **Elimination of asymmetry** between the influence of government and the people
