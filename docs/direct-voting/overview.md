# Direct Voting: Overview

## Definition

**Direct voting** is a mechanism in which every citizen personally and directly participates in political decision-making, without the mandatory intermediation of representatives. Unlike representative democracy, where citizens elect deputies who then vote on their behalf, direct democracy gives everyone the right to vote on every issue.

## Why Direct Voting Is Needed

The fundamental invariant property of democracy is **making authoritative decisions through voting**. Democracy is a system that:

1. **Determines the political will of the people** through decision-making via voting
2. **Implements adopted decisions** through elected officials

The purpose of voting is to **resolve conflicts with minimal losses** to society by numerically assessing opposing forces. Voting replaces a physical confrontation of sides with a more socially beneficial numerical comparison.

## Differences of the Proposed System

The proposed direct voting system differs from existing models in several key parameters:

### Compared to Traditional Representative Democracy

| Parameter         | Traditional                      | Proposed            |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Voting frequency  | Once every 4–7 years             | Continuous          |
| Subject of voting | Choosing a representative        | Every law and issue |
| Verification      | Trust in commissions             | Cryptographic       |
| Vote weight       | Equal                            | Competency-based    |
| Delegation        | No (only through representative) | Yes, with override  |

### Compared to Direct Democracy (e.g., Switzerland)

| Parameter             | Swiss Model                   | Proposed                  |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Referendum initiation | Petition signatures (complex) | Through the voting system |
| Frequency             | As initiated                  | Continuous                |
| Technology            | Ballots / electronic voting   | Blockchain + GPG          |
| Approval required     | Yes                           | No                        |
| Competency accounting | No                            | Yes                       |

## Key Components of the System

The direct voting system comprises several interconnected mechanisms:

- **[Continuous Voting](../voting-process/continuous-voting.md)** — the ability to vote at any time
- **[Inertia](../voting-process/inertia.md)** — a cumulative mechanism protecting against impulsive decisions
- **[Delegation](../voting-process/delegation.md)** — transferring your vote to a trusted person
- **[Blockchain & Cryptography](../voting-process/blockchain.md)** — technological foundation
- **[Competency Weights](../competency-weights/overview.md)** — accounting for professional competencies
