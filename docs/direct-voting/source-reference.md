# Source Reference: "Non-Revolutionary Development and Continuous Democracy"

## About the Article

This concept builds upon ideas presented in the scientific article:

> **Stefanov Ya.N.** "Non-Revolutionary Development and Continuous Democracy"
> Scientific-Literary Journal, No. 2(32)/2024
> Lomonosov Moscow State University

The article proposes a theoretical model of a democratic system capable of self-transformation during normal operation without revolutionary destruction. The author calls such a system **non-revolutionary**.

## Key Ideas from the Original Source

### The Driving Force Behind Revolutions

The author analyzes the prerequisites for revolutions according to Lenin and Sharp and concludes that **the key objective condition for an upheaval is the contradiction between the people and the government**. Neutralizing this condition completely solves the problem of revolutionary situations.

### Continuous Voting

To create a non-revolutionary system, the author proposes a **continuous voting** mechanism: a citizen can vote for a candidate on any working day. If the citizen previously voted for a different candidate, their vote is transferred. Competition between candidates continues constantly.

### Inertia Through Point Accumulation

To protect against impulsive decisions, the system uses **cumulative electoral point (EP) counting**:

- Each day, the difference between the candidate's and the incumbent's vote percentages is calculated
- The difference accumulates (integrates) day by day
- To win, a candidate must reach a certain threshold (e.g., 3,700 EP)
- If the difference becomes negative, points are subtracted

### Decisive Voting (Referendum)

The author describes the decisive voting procedure:

1. **Application and review** — the question is checked for compliance with formal requirements
2. **Selection and approval** — the question must gather support from 10% of voters
3. **Harmonization** — legal refinement of the wording
4. **Voting** — continuous voting with a participation threshold of 60%

## What Was Taken from the Original Source

The following were borrowed from Stefanov's article for our concept:

- ✅ **The concept of continuous voting** — citizens vote at any time
- ✅ **The principle of inertia** — a cumulative mechanism protecting against impulsive decisions
- ✅ **The idea of non-revolutionary change** — the system must allow fundamental changes without violence
- ✅ **Asymmetry analysis** — the gap between 0.1% influence of the people and 100% influence of the government

## What Was Changed and Added

Our concept significantly expands and modifies the original:

- 🔄 **Removed the need for law approval** — laws pass directly through continuous voting
- ➕ **Blockchain and GPG cryptography** — technological foundation for transparency and verification
- ➕ **Vote delegation** — the ability to delegate your vote to a trusted person with override rights
- ➕ **Competency weight system** — accounting for professional competencies in voting
- ➕ **Peer-based competency assignment** — professionals themselves determine each other's competency levels
- 🔄 **Digital implementation** — instead of physically visiting a "registration body," a cryptographic system is used
