# Continuous Direct Democracy

**Continuous Direct Democracy** is a concept for a political system in which citizens participate in decision-making continuously, rather than once every few years. The system is built on modern cryptographic technologies and ensures full transparency and verifiability of the voting process.

## Core Principles

1. **Continuous Voting** — a citizen can cast or change their vote at any time, without waiting for election day.
2. **Inertia** — the system is protected from impulsive decisions through a cumulative point-scoring mechanism.
3. **Cryptographic Verification** — every vote is recorded in an open blockchain network using GPG keys, allowing each citizen to verify that their vote was counted correctly.
4. **Delegation** — a citizen can delegate their vote to a trusted person while retaining the right to vote personally at any time.
5. **Competency Weights** — the votes of professionals in relevant fields carry greater weight when voting on specialized issues.
6. **No Approval Required** — laws pass through continuous voting without bureaucratic approval procedures.

## Origin of the Idea

This concept builds upon ideas presented in the scientific article by Ya.N. Stefanov, "Non-Revolutionary Development and Continuous Democracy" (2024). The concepts of continuous voting and system inertia were taken from the original source and significantly reworked and expanded.

Read more about the original source in the [Source Reference](./direct-voting/source-reference.md) section.
