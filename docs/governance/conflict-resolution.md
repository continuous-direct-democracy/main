# Conflict Resolution Between Laws

## The Problem

In a system where laws are adopted continuously by different groups of citizens, situations inevitably arise where **two laws define the same entities with different values**. For example:

- An existing law sets an import duty on imported goods at 50%
- A new law proposes an import duty of 10%

Such a conflict must be detected and resolved **before the new law takes effect**.

## Principle

**Two different laws cannot define the same entities without specifying priority.** If a conflict is detected, it must be resolved through one of the established methods.

## Automatic Conflict Detection

The system automatically analyzes each proposal and existing law for conflicts:

- **At the publication stage** — the automated system informs the author about potential conflicts (advisory; does not block publication)
- **At the support-gathering stage** — conflict information is published alongside the proposal so citizens can consider the context
- **Upon adoption** — the law **cannot take effect** with unresolved conflicts

:::warning Important
Conflicts **do not block** publication and voting — they only block **taking effect**. This gives the community time for discussion and choosing a resolution method during collaborative work on the law.
:::

## Resolution Methods

When a conflict is detected, the author and community must choose one of the following paths:

### 1. Amendment to the Existing Law

Make changes to the existing law on the conflicting point.

**Mechanics:**

- Submitted as a separate amendment proposal referencing the existing law
- Goes through the standard procedure: publication → collaborative work → gathering support → voting
- Both proposals (the new law and the amendment to the old one) can be voted on in parallel
- The new law takes effect only after the amendment to the old law is adopted

**Example:** A new law proposes a 10% duty. In parallel, an amendment to the old law is submitted, removing the 50% duty provision. Both are voted on. If both are adopted — the conflict is resolved.

### 2. Repeal of the Old Law

Initiate complete repeal of the existing law.

**Mechanics:**

- A proposal for complete repeal of the old law is submitted
- Goes through the standard voting procedure
- The new law takes effect only after the old one is repealed

**When appropriate:** when the old law is completely outdated and the new law is its comprehensive replacement.

### 3. Removal of the Conflicting Provision

Remove the disputed provision from the new law.

**Mechanics:**

- The author or community submits an amendment to the new law, removing the conflicting provision
- The amendment goes through the standard collaborative process
- After removing the conflicting provision, the law can take effect

**When appropriate:** when the conflicting provision is not central to the new law.

### 4. Explicit Priority Declaration

If the laws are at **different levels** of the hierarchy, the conflict can be resolved by declaring priority.

**Mechanics:**

- The new law explicitly states that it takes priority over a specific provision of the old law
- Priority is determined according to the current hierarchy (see below)
- If the new law's priority is higher — the conflict is resolved automatically

## Priority Hierarchy

Laws have priority levels depending on their scope:

| Level          | Priority | Example                       |
| -------------- | -------- | ----------------------------- |
| Constitutional | Highest  | Fundamental rights & freedoms |
| Federal        | High     | National laws                 |
| Regional       | Medium   | Regional/state laws           |
| Municipal      | Basic    | Local ordinances              |

**Priority rules:**

- A higher-level law takes priority over a lower-level law
- Laws at the same level **cannot conflict** — the conflict must be resolved through one of the methods above
- The current priority hierarchy **is itself subject to voting** and can be changed through the standard procedure

:::note Self-Modifiability
The rules of hierarchy and conflict resolution are **initial parameters** of the system. They can be reconsidered through the same continuous voting mechanism (see [Conclusion](../conclusion.md)).
:::

## Resolution Process

```
Conflict detected
       │
       ▼
┌──────────────────┐
│ Laws at different │──── Yes ──→ Priority by hierarchy
│ levels?           │
└──────────────────┘
       │ No
       ▼
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Choose resolution method:             │
│                                      │
│ 1. Amendment to existing law         │
│ 2. Repeal of old law                 │
│ 3. Remove provision from new law     │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
       │
       ▼
   Voting on chosen method
       │
       ▼
   Conflict resolved → New law takes effect
```

## What If the Conflict Is Not Resolved

If the community cannot reach a decision on the resolution method:

- The new law **remains under vote** but does not take effect
- Citizens continue to discuss and propose amendments
- There is no time limit — the conflict is resolved when society is ready
- The automated system continues to notify about the unresolved conflict
